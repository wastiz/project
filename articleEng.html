1. What start from?

I want to say that the creation of any site (especially commercial) worth a lot of effort and time. This is a long process which may drag on for months or even years. Therefore, such a decision needs take it seriously and responsibly. Unfortunately, you need accept that this is not an easy task, so I advise for all beginners draw up a detailed plan in which you write down all the goals, time and deadlines. This will help you stay on track and keep doing it. to end. Of course, you can hire a specialist in this matter or order services from a company that creates websites, and entrust all work for them. But we do not consider this option in this creative work


2. Idea

To begin with, of course, you need to decide on the idea and carefully think and work through. For whom and for what do I need to do? How will it look like? What should he be? What is his should it be functional? Are there competitors? What are their benefits? And a hundred more questions that you need to answer in order to continue develop your project. There are also many methods to help answer questions about the need, practicality of the site and complement your idea something else. Such methods are suitable for both large companies and one person who dreams of doing his own project. For example, Lean Canvas. Lean Canvas is a template for building a project model. The basis of the model is lean thinking philosophy and Lean Startup methodology. Lean canvas helps product manager or product owner to quickly describe your idea. Or, for example, there is another technique ‘How can I’. In this method, you must first come up with a question related to the idea of ​​your site that poses you a problem that the site can solve. And then you answer this question, thereby complementing the idea of ​​your site. You might even change your mind after all this. continue this idea and switch to another. But such a ‘brainstorm’ will help you present a general look and functionality, set specific goals and stick to them, changing only small details.


3. Feedback

So, you've carefully considered the idea. What is the best thing to do next? In my opinion, it would be best to interview people in order to collect feedback connection according to your idea and generally understand whether others need to visit your site. The most important thing in this case is to correctly complete the questionnaire. Ideal questionnaire is, of course, live. In this way, one can interrogate a person in detail and get the information you need and maybe even a recommendation. But this is again a costly business, so consider the usual questionnaire in Google. Generally do correct questionnaires, it is not as easy as it may seem, and there are people who who do it professionally. There are many videos on the internet such people from whom you can learn how to do it in the best way. But if we talk about the main thing in the questionnaire, then this is to clearly understand what you want learn from the interviewee. You can test your hypotheses about your project. These are the questions you might have asked at the idea development stage, but could not answer because there were no statistics. Through the survey, you can understand that your site will be useful or even complement your idea. When you do the questionnaire is best given to strangers, because they will is more critical and will represent potential users.


4. Name of the site

It would seem, what could be easier than choosing a site name? Nose this is where it all starts. Try telling this to business owners, well-known brands, popular brands or parents who choose name for your child. If you omit the pangs of choice, then always remain certain technical requirements for length and spelling. Necessarily account should be taken of geographical and other factors that may or otherwise affect the future fate of the site.


5. Site functionality

So, when we thought over the idea and came up with a name, we need to start think about what the site can do. Of course, you need to make functions for the necessary tasks, because if you add a bunch of unnecessary tools, buttons, features, then it will be very busy user (moreover, mentally from ignorance of how to navigate the site, as well as his computer). And what is most important is the development time and implementation of these functions and capabilities. For example, a chat with the administration or you directly. For some sites, this feature is categorically important, and, for example, for a site with a blog, it will be enough to indicate a link to the social. net. So, the functionality of the site needs to be worked out reasonably and counting all factors. Of course, this must be done at least on a piece of paper, drawing diagrams of how the site will respond to a particular user action. But since a lot of paper may be required and everything cannot fit, and not very it is convenient to do this when there are a bunch of platforms for such tasks. For example, the Whimsical platform. Whimsical is a visual working a space for reflection and collaboration that brings together flowcharts, wireframes, notes, mind maps and documents. Very useful web application for such tasks.
